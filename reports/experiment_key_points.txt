TIKHONOV IHCP SCIENTIFIC AGENT — KEY EXPERIMENT POINTS
=======================================================
Date: 2026-04-13
Basis: 4 benchmark cases (step flux, noise=0.1/0.5 K), all variants run

=== 5 MAIN FINDINGS ===

1. FIXED LAMBDA COMPLETELY FAILS
   fixed_solver (lambda=1.0) achieves 0% success rate across all 4 cases.
   flux_rmse = ~23,280 W/m2 (vs ~6,000 W/m2 for auto-lambda methods).
   replay_rmse = 2.30 K (fails the 2.0 K threshold in all cases).
   The solution is heavily over-regularized and collapses to a near-constant.
   Implication: automatic lambda selection is non-negotiable for IHCP.

2. FULL AGENT ACHIEVES 100% WEAK_PASS vs 25% FOR SINGLE-SHOT METHODS
   full_agent: 4/4 cases accepted (weak_pass).
   auto_solver and solver_plus_verifier: 1/4 cases each (25%).
   The gap is entirely driven by iterative replanning: the replanner detects
   oscillatory under-regularized solutions and corrects them in 1-3 steps.

3. REPLANNING MECHANISM WORKS AS DESIGNED
   For low noise (0.1 K), the discrepancy principle picks lambda ~1e-9,
   which is under-regularized. Replanner correctly fires switch_reg_order_to_2,
   then increases lambda 5x per step until weak_pass is reached.
   For medium noise (0.5 K), the discrepancy principle itself picks a good lambda
   and no replanning is needed (1 iteration, replanning_count=0).

4. VERIFIER CORRECTLY IDENTIFIES WEAK BUT PASSING SOLUTIONS
   auto_solver's last_verifier_decision is weak_pass in all 4 cases,
   even when the final_decision is fail (due to budget exhaustion mid-replan).
   This confirms the verifier is functioning correctly: it identifies the solution
   quality, and the replanner-budget interaction explains the discrepancy.

5. LOW RECONSTRUCTION ERROR DOES NOT GUARANTEE FORMAL ACCEPTANCE
   auto_solver achieves lower flux_rmse than full_agent in low-noise cases
   (4,886 vs 6,292 W/m2 for case_0001) but is not formally accepted.
   The full_agent trades a small amount of reconstruction accuracy for solution
   smoothness, which is required for acceptance. This tradeoff is expected.


=== 5 LIMITATIONS ===

1. EXTREMELY LIMITED BENCHMARK COVERAGE
   Only 4 of 30 planned cases have been run (13%).
   All 4 are the "step" flux family only. No ramp, pulse, or sinusoidal cases tested.
   Only noise=0.1 and 0.5 K tested; noise=1.0 K not yet run.
   Statistical conclusions are not yet possible.

2. NO ABLATION OR STRESS TEST RESULTS
   ablation_v1 and stress_v1 configs exist but zero runs have been executed.
   Cannot yet answer: which agent component contributes most?
   Cannot yet answer: where does the agent break down (few sensors, high noise, etc.)?

3. OSCILLATION SCORE IS IDENTICAL ACROSS ALL CASES (POSSIBLE BUG)
   oscillation_score = 0.3865546218487395 for every single row in results_raw.csv,
   regardless of variant, noise level, or solution quality.
   For fixed_solver (near-flat solution), this score should be much lower.
   The computation may be applied to the wrong array or have a logic issue.

4. DUPLICATE ROWS IN results_raw.csv
   case_0001 has 6 rows instead of 4: auto_solver and fixed_solver appear twice.
   This biases the summary statistics (n=5 for some variants vs n=4 for others).
   Needs to be cleaned before any final reporting.

5. ONLY SYNTHETIC DATA, ONLY TIKHONOV, ONLY 1D
   No real experimental data validation.
   No comparison with Bayesian methods, PINN, or classical iterative IHCP solvers.
   Only 1D homogeneous geometry with constant material properties.
   The "scientific agent" framing is validated at the workflow level only.
